## 1 · The Whole Truth, in One Page

Six hundred standard years ago, the **previous** Dawn of the Golden Spawn went catastrophically wrong on Lustriax Prime. The Golden One that hatched at the Resplendent Frond was unstable; its first uncontrolled psychic pulse **boiled the entire spawning vat** — every other hatchling in the brood was poached in an instant. *Eggs in the pan.* The whole wetland stayed superheated for a standard week, and survivors named the place **Lilypad Hell** that day.

The Astro Priests of that era could not destroy the surviving Golden One, so they did the next best thing: they sealed his unconscious body in a suit of **Alligator Warrior terminator armor**, fused his consciousness to a **single-seat antigrav hovercraft**, branded him with a Geas of Sealing, and set him loose to patrol the wetland forever, with one directive — *shepherd the next hatching, and personally execute the next Golden One if it too proves unworthy.*

This is **the Slerminator**.

He has not slept in six centuries. He still believes himself to be the rightful Golden One. He is here today.

His true name is **Cuitexotl-Tannin, the First-Hatched.** Speak it to him and he will hear you.

## 3 · The Three Endings

### The Peace Path *(the good ending)*
On any single turn after the Slerminator has appeared, **all three** of the following must happen with the Slerminator within 12" of the spawning vat:

1. **Both Astro Priests** complete the **Unsealing Rite** — joint Int test, +1 if either has witnessed any Slann ritual this game.
2. **Kozmox** (or any surviving Spawnfather) speaks the True Name **"Cuitexotl-Tannin, the First-Hatched"** — Cl test.
3. **Krookomet** (or any other Bull Slann officer) **volunteers as successor.** This is permanent. The volunteer is sealed into the armor and becomes the *next* Slerminator. Their next-session character is retired.

If achieved: the Slerminator dismounts, kneels, and powers down. The brood hatches safely. Closing voice-over plays. Photos.

> **GM fallback if no player will volunteer.** Don't force it. Offer two outs:
> (a) Any *named NPC* Bull Slann attached to a faction (e.g., Pimblar Ono, a Venom Tribe elder, one of Tepi's pinkies who turns out to be ex-Bull-Slann) steps forward — that player gives up the NPC instead of their PC.
> (b) The Slerminator demands flesh anyway: roll 1d6 for each Bull Slann hero on the table; lowest roll is dragged into the armor by the hovercraft's grapnel. The brood still hatches safely.
> In either case the player whose Bull Slann is taken keeps their character sheet — *they* are now the next Slerminator, ready to be re-skinned for next year's con.

### The Fight Path *(the brawl ending)*
The Slerminator can be killed. He must be reduced to 0 wounds **in a single turn** (his Cybernetic Regeneration restores 1 wound at the start of each of his turns unless he is reduced to zero in one turn). Realistically requires **three players coordinating**: concentrated plasma fire, a melta or krak charge, and at least one Slann hero in melee. If killed, the brood hatches safely but the surviving factions immediately re-roll initial objectives against each other for the final turn.

### The Bad Ending *(if the Spawn hatches and the Slerminator is still active and free)*
"Active and free" = on the board, above 0 wounds, and **not** powered down by the Peace Path. Roll 1d6 at the moment of hatching:

- **1–4** — The new Golden One is judged unworthy. The Slerminator vaporizes the entire vat — **table-wide S 6 hit, no saves, no cover**. Most of the table dies. Lilypad Hell stays Hell for another six centuries. *Cycle repeats.*
- **5–6** — The new Golden One passes. The Slerminator self-destructs in a 6" S 8 burst centered on himself. The faction with the most surviving figures within 6" of the vat afterward "wins."

## 4 · The Slerminator — Profile

| | **M** | **WS** | **BS** | **S** | **T** | **W** | **I** | **A** | **Ld** | **Int** | **Cl** | **WP** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Slerminator (dismounted) | 4 | 8 | 8 | 6 | 7 | 6 | 8 | 4 | 10 | 10 | 10 | 10 |
| Hovercraft (mount) | 18" | — | — | — | 8 | 4 | — | — | — | — | — | — |

When mounted, treat as a single combined model with M 18" and the Slerminator's other stats. The hovercraft can **turbo-boost to 36"** once per turn at the cost of all shooting that turn.

### Wargear
- **Psi-glaive** — S+3 power weapon, ignores all armor saves, +1 to hit in challenges.
- **Wrist-mounted plasma cannon** — S 9, range 24", 1 shot. Any roll of 1 to hit overheats and inflicts 1 wound on the Slerminator (saves allowed).
- **Terminator armor** — Sv **1+**. Any small-arms attack of S 4 or less only wounds on a natural 6 to wound; no save modifier applies.
- **Antigrav hovercraft** — Sv **2+** vs anything that targets the mount; the mount takes incoming hits unless the rider is specifically targeted.

### Special Rules
- **Causes Fear** — gleaming gold, two stories tall, six centuries of soot.
- **Psyker Mastery 4** — knows: *Cause Boiling* (S 5 hit on one target within 24"), *Hover Surge* (move full M after shooting), *Spawn-Sense* (knows location of every Slann hero on the table), *Geas of Sealing* (target friendly model is paralyzed for 1 turn — used by GM to set up the Peace Path).
- **Cybernetic Regeneration** — regrows 1 wound at the start of each of his turns *unless* reduced to 0 W in a single turn.
- **Ancient Geas** — cannot voluntarily leave a 24" radius of the central vat. If forced out, suffers 1 wound per turn until back inside.
- **Targeting Glyph Recognition** — any model wearing **Venom Tribe luminous toad-toxin paint** is treated as a friendly guardian and **will not be attacked first**. Once attacked by such a model, the truce ends for that unit only.
- **Single-Track Mind** — must always engage the unit nearest the central vat. Cannot be charmed, lured, or psyched away.

## 5 · Random Arrival

The Slerminator starts the game offboard, patrolling deep inside Lilypad Hell.

> At the **end of each turn from Turn 2 onward**, roll **2d6**. If the roll is **≤ the current turn number**, the Slerminator's hovercraft erupts from a frond-bog at a random board edge. He acts at the *start of the next turn* as an independent NPC unit and stays for the rest of the game. **If he has not appeared by the end of Turn 4, he arrives automatically at the start of Turn 5** — the players need at least three full turns with him on the table for the Peace Path to be feasible.

**Edge & distance:** roll 1d6 for board edge (1=N, 2=E, 3=S, 4=W, 5=NE corner, 6=SW corner). Roll 1d6 × 6" for distance from the corner along that edge.

**Movement priority:** beelines straight for the spawning vat, attacking anyone in his path or within 12" of the vat. See **Single-Track Mind**.

> **Hatching window.** The vat is scheduled to hatch at the **end of Turn 7**. If the Astro Priests are within 12" of the vat *and* chanting (any psychic action), the GM may delay hatching by one turn — once only. This buys the Peace Path one extra attempt.

## 6 · Per-Faction Victory Conditions

Each player has a **public objective** (on their player reference) and a **secret objective** (revealed only at end of session, used as the tiebreaker).

| Faction | Public objective | Secret objective (tiebreaker) |
|---|---|---|
| **Kozmox** | Celebrate the hatching of every clutch. | Survive the session with at least one named cousin alive. |
| **Tepi & Biglips** | Ink new performance contracts and evade the law. | Get at least one signed contract from any faction other than Krookomet's. |
| **Krookomet** | Prevent mayhem and maintain order. | Either arrest Tepi *or* be the volunteer in the Peace Path. |
| **Xochiton** | Witness all prophecies come to pass. | Be within 18" of the vat at the moment of hatching. |
| **Ninjorbit** | Permit no desecration of this sacred site. | Have at least three Venom Tribe models within 12" of the vat at hatching. |
| **Priests** | Take custody of all hatchlings as was foretold. | Successfully complete the Unsealing Rite (whether the Peace Path resolves or not). |

Score 1 point for completing your public objective, 1 point for the secret objective, and 1 point if your faction is in the Peace Path coalition. Highest score wins; ties are broken by who is closest to the vat.

## 8 · Hints to Feed the Players

Drop these in order, on cue. Don't volunteer them all — let the table earn most of them.

1. **First Slerminator turn.** Describe the gold leaf, the centuries of soot, and the *ancestral* faction-glyphs of all six current factions painted *over each other* on his armor. Hint: *"He's seen all of you before."*
2. **First time he passes a Venom Tribe model.** He visibly ignores it, even when it would be the closest target. Let the Ninjorbit player notice out loud.
3. **First "pinky" killed near Tepi.** That pinky's last word, in clear Imperial Gothic, is **"Tannisport."** Biglips immediately eats the corpse — but the table heard it.
4. **Any Astro Priest within 12" of the vat.** He involuntarily begins chanting words he didn't know he knew. → triggers the Unsealing Rite mechanic.
5. **Krookomet listens to the Slerminator's vox-channel.** It's a Bull Slann command-prayer in his own dialect, recited on loop. He recognizes it as the Alligator Warrior Ascension Rite.
6. **Anyone investigates a flooded shrine.** Inside is an old Bull Slann skeleton in cracked terminator armor — clearly welded *into* the suit. *"Ascension," indeed.*
7. **Xochiton, GM's option, once.** *"This has happened before. I came to see if it would happen again."*

### Leaking the True Name (use only if the table is stuck)
- Pimblar Ono blurts it from a vision (Xochiton's option).
- A Venom Tribe elder remembers it (Ninjorbit's option).
- A glitching pinky speaks it in Tannisport's voice.

### Never Tell the Players Outright
These must be *earned* on-table or the scenario collapses into trivia:

- That the Slerminator **is** a previous Golden One.
- That **Alligator Warrior** = welded-in Bull Slann officer.
- That **Kozmox's bloodline** is the next harvest if today goes wrong.
- That the **toad-toxin paint** is what's keeping Ninjorbit alive in the Slerminator's presence.
- The True Name. Players speak it; you only ever *confirm* it.

### Playing the Slerminator (GM voice notes)
- **Voice:** deep, vox-distorted, half a beat slow. Speak only in Bull Slann liturgical fragments until the True Name is spoken — then exactly **one** sentence of plain Imperial Gothic, quietly: *"...thank you. I had forgotten."*
- **Manner:** never *runs.* He drifts. He has all the time in the world; everyone else has six turns.
- **Targeting on arrival:** look at the table, ignore everyone for a beat, then point at the model nearest the vat. Move toward that model in a straight line. Do not negotiate. Do not threaten. Just arrive.

## 9 · The Resplendent Frond — Map Notes

- **Central spawning vat.** Golden, bubbling, centerpiece of the board. Hatching scheduled for the end of Turn 7 if the vat is not ruptured (see § 5 hatching window).
- **Six approach causeways.** One per faction, from each table edge, lined with luminous lilypad lights.
- **The frond canopy.** Partial cover (–1 to hit) over roughly 30% of the board.
- **Three flooded shrines.** Each contains a Bull Slann skeleton welded inside cracked terminator armor — previous failed Alligator Warrior candidates from earlier cycles.
- **Tannisport's overturned mess-cart.** Center-rear of the board, with a plasma-warped frying pan still chained to it. Flavor only — but if a player examines it, give them one free voice-over hint in Tannisport's voice.
