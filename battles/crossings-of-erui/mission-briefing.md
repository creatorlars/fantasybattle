# The Battle of the Crossings of Erui

## A scenario for *Warhammer Fantasy Battle 3rd Edition* — Lars Larsen & Chase Reinhart, 3D Roleplay Con VI, May 2026

> *"In the tenth year Eldacar, when men looked not for him, came out of the North with a great host, and people flocked to him from Calenardhon and Anórien and Ithilien. There was a great battle in Lebennin at the Crossings of Erui, in which much of the best blood in Gondor was shed."*
> — Appendix A, *The Lord of the Rings*

Ten years are gone since Castamir, Captain of the Ships, seized the Winged Crown by force, slew the king's son Ornendil at Osgiliath, and drove out lawful **Eldacar** — born **Vinitharya**, half of Númenor and half of the horse-lords of Rhovanion. In exile beyond Anduin the king has gathered his strength, and now in arms returns. As his banner came south through Calenardhon and Anórien and Ithilien, weary Gondor flocked to him; at his side ride **Aldamir** his son, sworn to avenge his murdered brother, and **Vidustain**, hot-blooded prince of Rhovanion, who would see his kinsman crowned within the gates of Osgiliath.

But the Usurper does not flinch. With his heir **Calimir** and the dread champion called only **The Brute**, **Castamir** has drawn up the marines of Pelargir, the southern corsairs, and the loyal spears and bows of Gondor at the fords of the **River Erui** in Lebennin. There the rivers of Westernesse blood will meet, and from this field one king shall ride away — and one shall not.

## Victory Conditions

*Each commander pursues his own ends. A player wins his personal contest by achieving a **majority** of his listed objectives.*

### Player 1 — King Eldacar  *(Host of Eldacar)*
- **Slay the Usurper.** Castamir must fall in battle — best of all by Eldacar's own hand.
- **Avenge Ornendil.** Either Calimir or The Brute must also be slain.
- **Hold the Crossings.** At game's end Eldacar's host controls the central ford (no enemy within 6"; a friendly unit in contact).
- **The King Returns.** Eldacar himself is alive and not Routed at game's end.

### Player 2 — Prince Vidustain  *(Host of Vidustain)*
- **Shield the Kinsman.** Eldacar must survive the battle.
- **Glory of the Plains.** Vidustain personally defeats a named enemy hero in close combat.
- **Hammer of Rhovanion.** Vidustain's host routs or destroys at least **two** enemy regiments.
- **Ride to the Gate.** At least one of Vidustain's mounted units ends the battle on the southern bank — the road to Osgiliath open before it.

### Player 3 — Castamir the Usurper  *(Host of Castamir)*
- **The Pretender Dies.** Eldacar must be slain; while he lives the crown is never secure.
- **Break the Northmen.** At least **half** of all enemy regiments are Routed, fled, or destroyed.
- **King Unbowed.** Castamir himself is alive and not Routed at game's end. *(Should he fall, his cause falls with him, whatever else is achieved.)*
- **Deny the Ford.** No enemy unit ends the battle on the southern bank of the Erui.

### Player 4 — Calimir, son of Castamir  *(Host of Calimir)*
- **Defend the Sire.** Castamir must survive the battle.
- **Heir's Valour.** Calimir personally slays a named enemy hero (Eldacar, Aldamir, Vidustain, or Vidurafin).
- **Marines of Pelargir.** Calimir's Corsair regiments inflict more casualties (in models removed) than they suffer.
- **Survive to Sail.** Calimir himself is alive and not Routed at game's end — for a living heir may yet rally Pelargir and Umbar.

## The Strategic Outcome

| Outcome | Condition |
|---|---|
| **Eldacar Triumphant** | Castamir is dead and Eldacar yet lives. The Kin-strife ends; the rightful king reclaims the throne. |
| **Castamir Holds the Crown** | Eldacar is dead and Castamir yet lives. The Usurper's line is secured for a generation. |
| **A House Divided** | Both kings survive, or both fall. Gondor is left bleeding and leaderless; Castamir's surviving kin flee south to Umbar, to nurse their grievance for centuries to come. |
