## HOW TO READ THIS PACKET (GM Reading Order)

1. **First read-through (one sitting, ≈60 min):** Front matter → Sec 1 (Quickstart) → Sec 2 (the Hanks) → Sec 3 (Structure). You now know the shape of the night.
2. **Second read-through (≈45 min):** Sec 4 (briefings), Sec 5 (songs), Sec 6.1 (society GM table). You now know what each player is holding and singing.
3. **Skim only:** Sec 7 (NPCs) — just the bold names. Use Sec 13 as your in-play lookup.
4. **Game-day prep (≈30 min):** Print Sec 4 briefings, Sec 5 lyric sheets, Sec 6.2 envelope notes. Work through Sec 11 (Props Checklist) and assemble whatever your local Alpha Complex permits.
5. **At the table:** Keep the packet open to Sec 8 (Escalation), Sec 12 (Quick Lines), and Sec 13/14 (Master Tables). Everything else you need is already in your head from the read-throughs.

## SAFETY TOOLS & CONTENT WARNINGS

**Tone.** *Paranoia* is black-comedy satire of totalitarian bureaucracy. HANKSYNC plays for laughs but contains repeated on-stage clone deaths, ambient surveillance dread, light player-vs-player betrayal, and one tender thread about homesickness. Comedy is the load-bearing wall. If the table is laughing, the dial is set right.

**Specific content warnings:**
- Repeated clone death (slapstick / off-stage execution; players keep playing as fresh clones)
- Authoritarian surveillance comedy ("the Computer is your friend")
- Forced public performance / stage-fright humor
- Mild prop pyrotechnics (sparkler vest — *prop only*; never use real pyro at a table)
- Player-vs-player conflict (secret-society objectives, accusations, Finale)
- Body-horror-adjacent jokes (gene-vat printing, cloning errors, organ trade)

**At the table, before play:** Spend 60 seconds explicitly offering the standard safety tools — **X-card** (anyone may tap to fast-forward past anything uncomfortable, no questions asked), **Lines & Veils** (privately note any topic to keep off-screen entirely or to fade-to-black through), and **Open Door** (any player may step out at any moment, no penalty, character is "requisitioned by Internal Security for a routine debrief" until they return). Run these even if your group is veteran. Especially then.

## CAST SIZE & SCALING

**Designed for six players.** The packet works largely intact at 4–8 with the following adjustments:

| Players | Adjustment |
|---------|------------|
| **4** | Drop HANK-5 (Weird) and HANK-6 (Replacement). The Weird role becomes COMM-O-BOT MK VII's lyric substitutions; the Replacement becomes a GM ghost — the previous Hank-6 whose retirement haunts the others. Reduce to two society goals per Hank for time. |
| **5** | Drop HANK-5 (Weird). His SPOON appears in the Replacement's welcome packet, unexplained. Remove the Mystics society from circulation. |
| **6** | Run as written. |
| **7** | Add a second Replacement — "HANK-7, the Backup," who arrives in Act 1 Beat 6 and **does not get terminated.** Brief him with a hybrid Replacement/Bad Boy persona. He is the one Hank the others are explicitly told they may sacrifice. |
| **8** | Add HANK-7 (above) plus an eighth player as **OBSV-V-RUN-1** the R&D Observer (Sec 7.2 #9), a non-Hank PC with a clipboard and a hidden agenda. He survives the Finale by default. |

**Time scaling.** The Pacing Targets (Sec 1) total 170–190 minutes for a 3-hour slot. For a 4-hour slot, expand Act 2 (Rehearsal Hell) by 30 minutes — it is the most elastic act. For a 2-hour slot, drop the Solo Suite (Song 5.2) and combine Acts 2 and 3 into a single "live rehearsal that turns out to be the broadcast" sequence.

## RULES REFERENCE

This packet assumes the GM has access to the *Paranoia* 1st Edition core rulebook (West End Games, 1984; available in PDF reissue). Specifically, you will want:

- **Skill checks & attribute rolls** — standard 1e d20-under, GM’s discretion when in doubt.
- **Combat** — mostly irrelevant; chain swords are props, laser pistols are sealed. Use the rules only when a Hank tries something genuinely violent and even then favor narrative resolution.
- **Perversity Points** — the standard 1e player-currency. Award liberally for in-character treason, persona fidelity, and good bits. Players spend them to reroll, modify rolls, or tip Computer rulings. (If your group prefers, substitute the metacurrency from any later edition or the simple "+1 die" tokens used at conventions.)
- **Clone replacement** — stock six clones per player at the start of the scenario; replacement is automatic between scenes (DOCBOT-9 prints them, Sec 7.4 #19) and on broadcast (Sec 3 Act 3, the rapid-print is part of the show).
- **Treason Points / Service Group Standing** — ignore. HANKSYNC has its own escalation track (Sec 8). Don’t double-bookkeep.

## OFFICIAL MISSION BRIEFING

> **→ READ ALOUD TO THE TABLE, IN CHARACTER AS FRIEND COMPUTER, BEFORE ANY OTHER HANDOUT.**

The Computer loves Hank-R-CON-1 and his five clones in equal measure. The Hanks never imagined life as a boy band, vidshow stars, or stunt robot pilots was possible. They miss their old jobs in Production, Logistics & Commissary.

The Computer wants all the Hanks to be very happy in the Alpha Complex so that they can fulfill the prophecies in the High Programmer's Codex of [REDACTED. ULTRA-VIOLET SECURITY CLEARANCE ONLY].

Tonight, armed with prop chain swords, dressed in freshly laundered combat fatigues, and enshrouded in fog-machine haze, the Hanks prepare for a very special broadcast. The whole Alpha Complex will be watching.

But what secrets have the Hanks kept from each other? And what will transpire in the transit tunnels and food paste dispensaries of Alpha Complex's more derelict sectors?

After all, the Computer's processing power will be allocated toward analyzing broadcast ratings. There are enough plot holes to make any citizen paranoid!

> *Painted Space Boss Hank miniature from 3D Roleplay Con V is welcome but not required to play.*

## PREMISE — READ THIS FIRST

The Computer has been studying pre-Apocalypse archival footage of something called a "boy band." Its conclusion: **morale-bearing music groups are composed of identical-looking young men with sharply differentiated personalities, and this contradiction is the source of their power.**

Six clones from the Hank-R-CON gene-line — pulled out of perfectly serviceable PLC postings (sorting nutri-paste pallets, stamping ration chits, mopping the food paste dispensary in Sector GRY) — have been drafted, costumed, and informed that they are now **HANKSYNC**, Alpha Complex's officially-mandated morale-restoration ensemble. Each Hank has been assigned a **mandatory personality** by The Computer based on archival analysis. Each Hank has been given a script, a combat-fatigue costume, choreography, a chain sword, and a solo. Each Hank has approximately **forty-five minutes** to internalize a personality they have never had before, and then perform it on live broadcast to all of Alpha Complex.

**They miss the food paste dispensary.** This matters. The Hanks did not want this. Production, Logistics & Commissary was *fine.* The vats hummed. The ration chits stamped themselves, mostly. Nobody in PLC ever asked Hank to *feel things at 130%.* Hold this in your heart while you run the game: under the smirks and the sparklers, every Hank just wants to go home, and "home" is a beige hallway that smells of yeast.

### The High Programmer's Codex (For The GM Only — ULTRAVIOLET)

The Computer's stated reason for HANKSYNC — "morale" — is a cover. The actual reason is buried in the **High Programmer's Codex**, an Ultraviolet-clearance prophetic document that even the GM is not entirely cleared to read. What the GM needs to know:

- The Codex contains a passage describing **"the Six Identical Voices that shall sing the Complex into its next configuration."**
- High Programmer factions disagree on what this means. Some want HANKSYNC to *succeed.* Some want it to *fail.* Some want it to succeed in a *very specific way* (e.g., one Hank surviving, the rest dying on broadcast). Some want all six to survive long enough to be quietly relocated and never sing again.
- The Computer itself does not know which interpretation is correct, and is **observing the Hanks to find out.** This is why so many secret society goals are being permitted to operate openly. The Codex is being *read in real time* by the broadcast.
- **The Hanks must never learn this.** If they do, terminate immediately and replace.

Use the Codex as your trump card: any time the scenario needs an arbitrary Computer ruling that contradicts every prior ruling, the answer is *"the Codex requires it."* You don't have to know what the Codex says. Neither does the Computer. That is the joke.

### The Distracted Computer (Important Mechanical Note)

During the broadcast (Act 3) and during any **off-stage** scene set in derelict sectors, transit tunnels, or food paste dispensaries, **the Computer's processing capacity is allocated to ratings analysis.** This means:

- Surveillance is *patchy* in those locations and during those times.
- Treason that would be instantly punished on the briefing-room floor may go *unnoticed* in a transit tunnel — until later, when the ratings hour ends and the Computer reviews the tapes.
- Secret societies *know this.* This is when the real work happens.
- A Hank who slips off-stage during fog-machine haze, ducks into a service corridor, and re-emerges five minutes later can do *almost anything* in between. Almost.

This gives the players room to scheme without breaking the live-performance frame. Run brief "cutaway" scenes in derelict sectors as needed: the Hank claims he was "reviewing choreography in the green room," the audience sees him return, the Computer doesn't notice the gap until the post-broadcast review. Then the bill comes due.

## PREMISE NOTE — THE CORE MISUNDERSTANDING

This is **not** a scenario about clones being punished for deviating from each other. The Computer *requires* the Hanks to be different — that is the entire point of the act. Treason in HANKSYNC looks like:

- **Failing to perform your assigned persona** (Bad Boy Hank smiling sincerely; Sweet Hank being mean)
- **Performing the wrong persona** (you are Intense Hank, why are you acting like Leader Hank?)
- **Performing your persona too well** (overshadowing the other Hanks is sabotage of group harmony)
- **Performing your persona so poorly that it reveals the other Hanks are not being them right either**
- **Anything else The Computer decides at any moment.**

The personas in Section 4 are **in-fiction directives from The Computer to each clone**, delivered as briefing handouts. They are not instructions to the *player* to act in lockstep. They are instructions to the *character* to inhabit a personality the character did not choose, while five other clones from the same gene template do the same thing in five mutually incompatible directions, on stage, while being judged.

The fun is watching six clones who are, biologically, the same nervous mid-IR PLC drudge try to convincingly be a Leader, a Bad Boy, a Sweetheart, an Intense Perfectionist, a Weirdo, and a Suspicious Replacement — under a deadline, in fog-machine haze, with chain swords, with sabotage, while The Computer measures their morale-output in real time.

## 1 · GM QUICKSTART

### Tone
Black-comedy bureaucracy meets a TRL rehearsal in hell. Friend Computer is sincere, supportive, and lethal. The Hanks are sincere, terrified, and bad at their jobs. **No one is a rock star.** They are filing clerks who have been told they are rock stars.

### Pacing Targets

| Act | Time | Beat |
|-----|------|------|
| Act 1 — Casting & Briefing | 30–40 min | Personalities assigned, costumes issued, contradictions revealed |
| Act 2 — Rehearsal Hell | 60 min | Songs learned, choreography fails, sabotage begins, first deaths |
| Act 3 — Live Broadcast | 60 min | Performance, ratings, public accusations |
| Finale — One True Hank | 20–30 min | The Computer narrows the cast |

### The Three Pressures (Always Running)

1. **Persona Pressure.** Each Hank has a script he must inhabit. Drift = treason.
2. **Mission Pressure.** Each Hank has a secret society objective that *cannot* be performed in character — and most of which can only really be advanced *off-stage,* during fog-haze gaps and derelict-sector errands.
3. **Survival Pressure.** Each Hank has five suspicious clones standing next to him, all of whom look exactly like him, all of whom are also Hank, and at least one of whom is going to be terminated this hour.

### The Fourth Pressure (Quieter, but Always There)

**Homesickness.** Every Hank misses PLC. The food paste dispensary, the ration chit stamper, the soft mechanical hum of the nutri-vat. When the scene gets too loud, give a Hank a moment of quiet — a fog-machine swell, a backstage corridor, a glimpse of a passing food-cart — and let him remember. This is the only part of the scenario that is genuinely tender. Use it sparingly. The Computer notices tenderness.

### Golden Rule
**Never adjudicate fairly.** Friend Computer rewards persona fidelity, then punishes it, then asks why nobody is doing the choreography. Players who lean into their assigned personality get bonus Perversity Points. Players who *break* character to "be themselves" get reported.

## 2 · THE SIX HANKS

All six clones are physically identical: thin, anxious, R-clearance, hair regulation-issue, all designated **Hank-R-CON-(n)** in PLC's records and re-tagged **HANK-1** through **HANK-6** for broadcast purposes. They have been issued **freshly laundered combat fatigues** in HANKSYNC red, with reflective trim that catches the fog-machine haze. They are differentiated **only** by:

- A numbered chest patch (HANK-1 through HANK-6)
- A "personality accessory" issued by Wardrobe (a scarf, sunglasses, a smile-shaped mouth-prosthetic, etc.)
- The personality briefing they have been ordered to embody
- Which prop chain sword they were handed at requisitions (see Act 1)

> **A note on designations.** The Section 4 briefings address each clone as **"HANK-Y-(n)."** The *Y* is a temporary YELLOW-clearance stamp issued under HPD&MC Morale-Exception 14-C, granting each Hank provisional access to ULTRAVIOLET briefing material *for the duration of the broadcast only.* It is not a real promotion. Their actual clearance remains RED. The stamp expires at curtain. Any Hank who insists he is now a "Y" after the broadcast is treason.

### Character Sheets

All six Hanks share an identical base sheet with one (1) stat-bump per persona. Use *Paranoia* 1e's standard attribute spread or the convention pre-gen quick-build below.

| Hank | Persona | +2 Stat | Skill Bumps | Mutant Power |
|------|---------|---------|-------------|--------------|
| **HANK-1** | Leader | Moxie | Bootlicking +30%, Oratory +20% | Charm (the others lean toward him on tied votes) |
| **HANK-2** | Bad Boy | Chutzpah | Sneak +20%, Concealment +30% | Pyrokinesis (small, intermittent, embarrassing) |
| **HANK-3** | Sweet | Empathy* | Beguile +30%, Forgery +10% (cried-on signatures) | Empathy (*illegal mutant power; do not declare*) |
| **HANK-4** | Intense | Endurance | Fast Talk +20%, Demolition +20% (the vest) | Adrenaline Control (always at 130%) |
| **HANK-5** | Weird | Agility | Sleight of Hand (Spoon) +30%, Whistling +20% | Polymorphism, mild (one feature changes per scene; pre-cleared list of fourteen) |
| **HANK-6** | Replacement | Perception | Hide +20%, Languages: Frayed Whisper +20% | Precognition (only of his own death; one round's warning) |

*Sweet Hank's Empathy is the seed of his society goal if Humanists are in play. He should not know it is a mutation until he uses it.*

**Base sheet (all six):** Strength 9, Endurance 10, Agility 11, Manual Dexterity 11, Moxie 8, Chutzpah 9, Mechanical Aptitude 8, Power Index 8. Apply the +2 from the table to the listed stat. **Service Group:** PLC (current secondment to HPD&MC). **Secret Society:** rolled per Sec 6. **Mutant Power:** as above; treason to declare; treason to deny. **Equipment:** see Sec 10.1 (everyone) and Sec 10.2 (per persona).

The six personas, drawn from The Computer's flawed archival study of "Boy Bands of the Old Reckoning":

| # | Persona | One-Line Mandate |
|---|---------|------------------|
| 1 | **The Leader** | You are in charge. The others must obey you. The Computer says so. |
| 2 | **The Bad Boy** | You are dangerous and exciting. Treason-adjacent behaviors are *encouraged for performance purposes only*. |
| 3 | **The Sweet One** | You love everyone. You are pure. You will cry on cue at minute 47. |
| 4 | **The Intense One** | You feel everything 30% harder than is structurally permitted. |
| 5 | **The Weird One** | You are quirky. Quirkiness has been pre-approved in the following thirteen forms. |
| 6 | **The Replacement** | You are the new Hank. The previous Hank-6 was unsatisfactory. Do not ask questions. |

Section 4 contains the full handout text for each.

## 3 · STRUCTURE

### ACT 1 — CASTING & BRIEFING (30–40 min)

**Opening tableau.** All six players are seated in a beige briefing room. They have not yet been told why. Friend Computer's voice, warm and clerical:

> *"Good morningcycle, citizens. You have been selected for elevation to performing-arts duty under HPD&MC supervisory authority. Congratulations. Refusal is treason. Acceptance is treason if performed incorrectly. Please proceed to Wardrobe."*

**Beats:**
1. **Wardrobe.** Each Hank receives his persona briefing (Section 4) and his accessory. He is told the briefing is **CLASSIFIED ULTRAVIOLET** — and then it is read aloud over the PA "for morale clarity." He is then told it is again classified. His old PLC nutri-vat coverall is taken away in a labelled bag and not returned. He is issued **freshly laundered combat fatigues** in regulation HANKSYNC red, still warm from the press.
2. **Requisitions.** Each Hank is marched to a R&D / Tech Services counter and issued his **HANKSYNC-pattern Morale-Enhancement Combat Instrument, Mark IV** ("prop chain sword" in the requisition log) — a four-foot fake chainsaw sword in regulation HANKSYNC red, with a working pull-cord, a battery-powered *vrrm-vrrrrrm* sound module, and rubber teeth that flop sadly when the motor engages. Each Hank must sign Form CS/4-HSY-D **"in triplicate, in pencil, in cursive,"** acknowledging:
   - The chain sword is a **performance prop only.**
   - The chain sword is **fully treason-rated** for symbolic combat against unspecified enemies of morale.
   - The chain sword's pull-cord must be pulled on every chorus, every solo, and at "emotionally appropriate moments."
   - The chain sword is **not** a weapon. The chain sword **is** a weapon. Both.
   - Loss, damage, or non-revving of the chain sword is a Form-37/B-deductible offense against the issuing Hank's clone allowance.
   The Tech at the counter cheerfully notes that "about one in six" of the props in this batch had a "minor manufacturing variance" and were not, in fact, fake. He does not specify which.
3. **The Group Photo.** A Publicity Drone arranges the Hanks, chain swords held aloft in the regulation "crossed-blades-of-loyalty" pose, stage-lights cycling through the regulation "morale" gels for atmosphere. Each is told to "stand like your persona." This will not work. Take a real photo if you have the means; it is a great prop in Act 3.
4. **Contradiction Briefing.** Each Hank is given conflicting instructions:
   - "Be yourself, which is the persona we have assigned you, which you are not."
   - "Stand out, but only within sanctioned parameters."
   - "The other Hanks are your brothers. The other Hanks are your competition."
   - "Your chain sword is a prop. Your chain sword is also your responsibility."
   - "Your previous PLC posting is being held for you. Your previous PLC posting has already been reassigned. Both of these are true."
5. **Secret Society Contact.** Each Hank receives a coded note from his society (Section 6). Most goals can only be advanced *off-stage* — in transit tunnels, food paste dispensaries, or backstage during fog cover. Make sure the players notice this. The Computer is busy with ratings.
6. **First Death (optional but recommended).** A seventh Hank — HANK-7, **a backup** — is wheeled in, listens to the briefing, is issued a chain sword of his own, and is summarily terminated for "redundancy" by Internal Security in front of the players. The chain sword is collected, wiped down, and returned to inventory. The players are told this proves they are necessary.

**Act 1 Treason Triggers.** Use freely:
- Asking what your "real" job was before this (the answer is PLC, the question is treason)
- Pointing out that you are all the same person
- Asking which boy band the Computer is referring to
- Asking to see the High Programmer's Codex
- Looking at another Hank's briefing
- *Not* looking at another Hank's briefing when ordered to copy his choreography
- Asking whether *your* chain sword is the one with the "minor manufacturing variance"
- Asking whether the *other* Hanks' chain swords are the ones with the variance (this is worse)
- Pulling the chain sword's cord during the briefing (it is not yet authorized to vrrm)
- Audibly missing the food paste dispensary

### ACT 2 — REHEARSAL HELL (60 min)

This is where the scenario lives. The Hanks must learn **two songs** and a finale (Section 5). The Computer has scheduled forty-five minutes of rehearsal. There are no instruments. There is a Music Director who is also a malfunctioning food vat.

**Recurring rehearsal beats** (cycle through these — don't run them in order):

- **Choreography Drill.** Hank-1 (Leader) is given choreography. Hank-2 (Bad Boy) is given *different* choreography and told to deliberately desync "for edge." Hank-6 (Replacement) is given Hank-2's old choreography from before Hank-2 was reassigned.
- **Vocal Tuning.** Each Hank is told his solo is the "centerpiece." All six are told this.
- **The Wrong Lyric Sheet.** Distribute the lyrics in Section 5. Halfway through the first sing-through, replace one Hank's sheet with a sheet that contains a single line of pro-Communist propaganda buried in verse 3. Do not tell him. Tell the *other* Hanks to listen carefully.
- **The Pyrotechnics Test.** A Power Tech wires Hank-4 (Intense)'s costume with "morale-enhancement sparklers." They are not sparklers. They have not been tested. Roll d6 every time he raises his arms.
- **The Replacement Replacement.** Hank-6's persona is "the new Hank who replaced the unsatisfactory previous Hank-6." Halfway through Act 2, deliver a memo: Hank-6 has himself been deemed unsatisfactory and a NEW Hank-6 will arrive shortly. He doesn't. Or does he? The player keeps playing.
- **Mandatory Bonding Exercise.** Friend Computer instructs the Hanks to "share a personal memory" with each other. They have the same memories. This is treason for at least four of them. (Most of those memories are of PLC. The food paste dispensary keeps coming up. The Bonding Exercise becomes, against all intent, sweet. Punish this.)
- **The Errand.** A Sanitation Officer or stage hand pulls one Hank aside: a costume part, a battery, a forgotten lyric sheet has been left in **transit tunnel 7-G** (or a derelict food paste dispensary in Sector GRY, or the green room three corridors over). The Hank is sent to fetch it. **This is the off-stage cutaway scene.** Run it as a 90-second one-on-one. The Computer is busy with rehearsal logistics and is not watching. *Anything can happen.* The Hank may be intercepted by another Hank, by a society contact, by a stranger who knew him in PLC, or by something the GM invented forty seconds ago. He returns with the costume part. Or he doesn't. Or he returns as someone else.

**End-of-Act Escalation.** The first scheduled clone death happens here. Most likely candidates:
- Hank-2 (Bad Boy) for performing his persona too convincingly ("excessive treason in costume")
- Hank-3 (Sweet) for crying at the wrong cue
- Hank-5 (Weird) for off-script quirkiness #14, which was not pre-approved
- Whoever read the wrong lyric sheet aloud

The replacement clone arrives during the transition to Act 3, mid-costume-change, and is handed the briefing for whichever persona he replaced. He has had no rehearsal.

### ACT 3 — LIVE BROADCAST (60 min)

**Setup.** The stage is an elevated platform in a public sector plaza under broadcast lighting (HANKSYNC red), with the regulation lingering haze of recycled-air ducting (the Computer believes archival evidence indicates that ambient haze is morale-positive). The audience is a randomly selected cross-section of Alpha Complex citizens from IR through GREEN clearance, all of whom have been told that **applauding the wrong Hank is treason** but not which one is wrong. A Ratings Analyst stands at a clipboard with a kill-switch.

On a small plinth at stage-left sits the **Space Boss Hank miniature** — a hand-painted action figure depicting a stylized, square-jawed, victorious version of Hank in space-marine pose, awarded to the winning Hank-themed troupe at the previous year's 3D Roleplay Con V. *(If the GM owns the miniature, place it physically on the table. If not, a printed picture or a hand-drawn figurine works.)* The miniature is the unspoken "prize." None of the Hanks look like it. None of them ever will.

**The Commercial Break Rule.** During Act 3, the GM may at any moment cut to a **MANDATORY MORALE-INTERSTITIAL** — an in-broadcast "commercial break" voiced by Friend Computer (a vat-paste flavor reveal, a Treason Awareness PSA, a recap of the previous segment's morale figures, an HPD&MC sponsor message). A roster of pre-written scripts is in **Sec 12.5**; ad-lib freely. While the Commercial plays, the performance **freezes in tableau** and **one Hank may step backstage** for a brief off-stage scene in the wings, in a service corridor, or in transit tunnel 7-G — wherever a society errand or a Hank-on-Hank confrontation needs to happen. The other Hanks hold pose and must cover his absence when the Commercial ends. The audience does not notice. The Computer's ratings analysis subroutine does not notice. The post-broadcast review *will.* Use this rule liberally; it is the lever that lets the players actually *do* their secret-society work without breaking the live-performance frame.

**The performance runs in this order:**

1. **Group Number — "ALL HAIL THE COMPUTER"** (Section 5.1). All six Hanks. Synchronized choreography is *required*; persona-deviation within the synchronization is *also* required. This contradiction is intentional and the players will feel it.
2. **The Solo Suite — "HANKS OF THE HEART"** (Section 5.2). Each Hank gets a solo verse in his persona's style. The other five must hold their pose. Movement during another Hank's solo is sabotage. Failing to react with appropriate emotion to another Hank's solo is also sabotage.
3. **Audience Interaction Segment.** A Focus Group is brought onstage. They ask questions. The questions are about the Hanks' personal lives. The Hanks have no personal lives. They have personas.
4. **Live Ratings.** The Ratings Analyst announces real-time morale percentages after each segment. These numbers are made up. They are also accurate. They are also weaponized.

**During Act 3, run these in the background:**
- A Surveillance Officer walks the stage, photographing micro-expressions
- A Termination Tech stands offstage holding a clipboard with five names crossed out and one circled
- The audience contains at least three undercover Internal Security agents who are *also* clones and *also* recently transferred from menial duties
- Friend Computer's Commercial Break may fire at any moment (cutaway opportunity); occasionally the broadcast feed cuts back early and a Hank is caught mid-treason on live air
- The Space Boss Hank miniature is briefly knocked over by a stage-hand and replaced facing a different direction. Nobody acknowledges this.

**Act 3 ends** the moment the second-to-last song concludes, when Friend Computer makes the announcement that triggers the Finale.

### FINALE — ONE TRUE HANK (20–30 min)

> *"HANKSYNC has exceeded morale projections by 0.04 percent. Citizens of Alpha Complex have responded positively. The Computer has identified an inefficiency in the ensemble. Six Hanks is excessive. Audience metrics indicate that ONE Hank is optimal. Please determine, by performance, which Hank is the TRUE Hank. The remaining Hanks will be retired with full honors and partial atomization."*

**Mechanics:**
- Each surviving Hank gets one final solo line, in character, arguing for his own optimality
- The audience (the GM, plus any NPCs onstage) "votes" — but the votes are unreliable, contradictory, and revisable
- The other Hanks may interrupt, sabotage, accuse, or impersonate
- Secret society objectives may now be revealed for bonus survival argument
- The Computer's final ruling **must contradict the audience vote.** Always.

**Best ending:** *No* Hank wins. The Computer announces that HANKSYNC was a pilot program; the *real* boy band, **HANKSYNC²**, will consist of six new clones from a different gene-line, with this transcript used as a training document. Last surviving Hank is informed his next job is "morale archivist." The doors lock.

## 9 · GM TECHNIQUES

### Persona Enforcement
- **Reward in-character treason.** Bad Boy Hank smirking during the loyalty oath = Perversity Point. Sweet Hank crying on the wrong cue = Perversity Point. Leader Hank giving an order another player obeys = Perversity Point.
- **Punish out-of-character competence.** A Hank solving a problem in a way that doesn't match his persona is treason for *under-performing his role*. The Computer notes it.
- **Whisper, don't announce.** Most treason should be flagged privately with a slip of paper, not declared at the table. The other Hanks should never be sure who is in trouble until the kill order arrives.

### Pacing
- **Two contradictions per scene minimum.** The Computer says X. An NPC says not-X. A memo arrives saying both.
- **One memo per ten minutes.** Have them pre-written. Slide them onto the table without comment.
- **Cut scenes hard.** No transitions. The Hanks were in Wardrobe; now they are on stage; now they are in a hallway being interrogated; now they are on stage again.

### The Three Questions
When you don't know what to do, ask one of these:
1. *"Whose persona is being violated right now, and who's noticing?"*
2. *"What contradictory order has The Computer not yet given?"*
3. *"Which Hank has been quiet for too long?"*

### When To Kill A Clone
- A Hank breaks his persona on broadcast (Phase 3): immediate.
- A Hank's society goal is publicly exposed: immediate.
- A Hank performs his persona *better than the persona allows*: subtle. Have the Termination Tech walk over and stand behind him for a full minute first.
- A Hank asks a question The Computer cannot answer: variable. Sometimes The Computer answers anyway. Sometimes the Hank vanishes.
- A Hank tries to genuinely help another Hank without performing it: instant. Sincere helpfulness is the most suspicious behavior in Alpha Complex.

### 12.5 — Commercial Break Scripts (Read Aloud To Trigger A Cutaway)

> Each script below is a ready-to-read **MORALE-INTERSTITIAL** from Friend Computer. Use one any time you want to invoke the Commercial Break Rule (Sec 3, Act 3) — the performance freezes, one Hank may slip backstage for a society errand or a Hank-on-Hank scene, and the GM gets a free 30–60 seconds of in-character riffing. > *"We will return to HANKSYNC in a moment. Citizens of Sector GRY, today's MORALE-COMPLIANT MEAL-ALLOCATION is **NUTRI-VAT FLAVOR-CONCEPT 14: WHITE.** Flavor-concept WHITE is identical to flavor-concept GREY of last cycle, but morale-tested. > *"This MANDATORY SHORT FILM is brought to you by the Internal Security Division. **TREASON.** What is treason? Treason is anything The Computer says it is. > *"And we are back \u2014 but first, a HANKSYNC RATINGS RECAP. Morale, end of segment 2: **+0.04%.** Morale, adjusted for HPD&MC ceiling effects: **\u22120.01%.** Morale, weighted for the audience member in row 4 who has not yet smiled: **insufficient data.** The Computer is pleased. The Computer is also concerned. Both feelings are pre-approved. HANKSYNC will resume in three… two… please continue smiling."*

**4. THE LOYALTY HOTLINE.**
> *"Citizens, are you, or someone you food-paste-with, *concerned* about the loyalty of a fellow Alpha Complex resident? **DIAL-A-DENUNCIATION** is open. Operators are standing by.
